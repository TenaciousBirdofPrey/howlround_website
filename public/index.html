Brendan O'Donnell

a.k.a

howlround

Brendan O'Donnell is a singer/songwriter living in Providence RI.


Performing under the name Howlround since 2004, he released his first album "Deep in The Valley Looking Down"

As a native of North Carolina, he grew up both influenced by the traditional acoustic Appalachian music and Irish Folk music of his grandparents. Not to be too fascinated with the past, he has forged his influences into a new digital abstraction ripe for fresh, creative manipulation.

Having come up in the same collective creative explosion of Greenville, NC that created The Avett Brothers, Valient Thorr and Future Islands Brendan was both a writer and musician and received the North Carolina's Writers Network Scholarship.

He put his musical feet on the ground with the now legendary punk band Lo-Fi Conspiracy and then went on to be an initial member of Valient Thorr. He became the drummer of the heralded Read Palms and then formed The Blessed (StickFigure Records)

He has toured extensively and shared the stage with everyone from Nemo (pre-Avett Bros) to Wesley Willis. He combines a fluctuating sound of abstract experimentation with hardened tradition through a loyal emphasis on lyric and storytelling.
